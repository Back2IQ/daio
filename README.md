## What is DAIO?

**DAIO** (Digital Asset Inheritance Orchestration) is a governance framework
that enables family offices, private banks, notaries, tax advisors, and
wealth management firms to implement structured digital asset inheritance
services — without holding client assets in custody.

It addresses a €800+ billion problem: digital wealth that becomes permanently
inaccessible because traditional estate planning cannot transfer private
keys, credentials, or digital identities.

## Why This Exists

- An estimated 20% of all Bitcoin is permanently lost due to missing
  succession infrastructure.
- **BGH III ZR 183/17** obligates financial institutions to disclosure duties
  for digital assets — even without custody.
- **MiCA** tightens documentation, traceability, and transfer requirements.
- Advisors face **personal liability** when inheritance processes are
  undocumented.
- The baby boomer generation reaches inheritance age between 2025 and 2035.

Yet most institutions have no standardized process for digital asset
succession. DAIO is the operational playbook that fills this gap.

## Key Concepts (Originated in This Work)

- **Sovereign Guard Architecture** — the no-custody governance model
- **Inheritance Container** — the core succession unit
- **Legacy Proof Protocol** — cryptographic proof-of-process for inheritance
- **Succession Sentinel System** — automated life-event detection
- **Transfer Gate Protocol** — authorization gateways for asset release
- **Heritage Integrity Framework** — compliance and audit integration

These terms are original to this work. See [NOTICE.md](NOTICE.md).
