## Sovereign Guard Architecture

**One principle: Orchestration without custody. Governance without liability.**

| | Custody-Based | Sovereign Guard |
|---|---|---|
| Key Custody | Centralized | With the owner |
| Liability | High (loss risk) | Low (no custody) |
| Regulation | CASP license required | No CASP license |
| Capital Requirements | Significant (MiCA) | None |
| Attack Vector | Central custodian | Decentralized |
| Control | With custodian | With owner |

The institution orchestrates the governance. The client keeps the keys.
No custody license. No capital requirements. Immediate market entry.

## The Inheritance Container

An encrypted, self-contained data space containing all succession-relevant information.

**What it holds:**
- Asset inventory (wallets, exchanges, DeFi, NFTs, domains)
- Heir designations with roles and allocations
- Access architecture (how to reach each asset)
- Trigger conditions (when succession activates)
- Professional contacts (notary, attorney, advisor)

**How it works:**
1. Owner fills container with structured data
2. Container is encrypted with AES-256-GCM
3. Encryption key is split via Shamir's Secret Sharing (k-of-n)
4. Shards distributed to heirs, guardians, notary
5. Every change appended to a tamper-evident hash chain

**No server. No blockchain. No platform dependency. Everything local.**

## Legacy Proof Protocol

Traditional succession: Heirs must **prove** their entitlement. Weeks. Months. €50,000–200,000.

DAIO: Entitlement is **documented during the owner's lifetime**.

| | Traditional | Legacy Proof Protocol |
|---|---|---|
| Time to Transfer | Weeks to months | Hours to days |
| Burden of Proof | Heirs must prove | System documents entitlement |
| Costs | €50,000–200,000 | 70–90% lower |
| Dispute Potential | Significant | Minimal |

Every container change is cryptographically recorded in the proof chain.
The chain is tamper-evident: alter one entry and the entire chain breaks.

---

## Succession Sentinel (Dead Man's Switch)

Continuous monitoring of the asset owner's status with automatic escalation.

**4-Stage Escalation:**
1. Warning 1 — First missed check-in
2. Warning 2 — Continued absence
3. Warning 3 — Final notice to all contacts
4. Triggered — Succession process initiates

Each check-in is recorded on the proof chain as **proof of life**.
Check-ins double as **container reviews** — the living container stays current.

---

## Transfer Gate Protocol

Standardized approval processes for every asset transfer.

**5 Validation Steps:**
1. Authorization — Heir identity verification
2. Verification — Multi-party key fragment confirmation (Shamir)
3. Preparation — Asset inventory review
4. Execution — Controlled transfer to beneficiaries
5. Completion — Audit trail and documentation

Every step is logged. Every decision is traceable. Fully audit-ready.

# CASE STUDIES

## Case 1: Family Office — €23.4M Crypto Portfolio

**Situation:** Bergmann family, Munich. €180M total assets, €23.4M in crypto (BTC, ETH, DeFi). Patriarch suffers mild stroke. Family discovers: no succession plan for digital assets. Seeds scattered. No documentation.

**Solution:** Full implementation of Sovereign Guard Framework. 8 weeks asset discovery (47 wallets, 7 exchanges, 3 DeFi positions). Inheritance containers created per asset category. Legacy Proof Protocol executed. New will drafted linking to containers.

**Result:**
- €23.4M fully documented and secured
- Succession Sentinel monitoring activated
- Project cost: €85,000 (0.36% of secured assets)
- Family gained complete certainty about digital wealth structure

---

## Case 2: Deceased Early Adopter — Posthumous Recovery

**Situation:** Thomas W., early Bitcoin adopter since 2011. Dies at 52. Estimated €2.8M in crypto across unknown wallets. Sole heir: 78-year-old mother. No documentation.

**Solution:** Forensic analysis of devices, emails, browser history. Identified 3 previously unknown wallets. Posthumous inheritance container created. Modified Legacy Proof Protocol with increased verification.

**Result:**
- €2.38M recovered (85% of estimated total)
- Transfer to heir completed in 8 weeks
- Project cost: €45,000 (1.9% of secured assets)

**Lesson:** Posthumous intervention works but costs 3–5x more than lifetime planning.

---

## Case 3: Corporate BTC Treasury — Key Person Risk

**Situation:** NordCap AG, industrial holding, 450 employees. CFO holds sole access to €4.2M Bitcoin treasury reserves. CFO suffers heart attack, out for months. Treasury inaccessible.

**Solution:** Forensic reconstruction of 3 of 4 wallet addresses. Succession Sentinel configured for incapacity scenarios. Transfer Gate with multi-level approval (deputy CFO up to €500k, CEO above, Supervisory Board above €1M).

**Result:**
- Access restored. New treasury manual created.
- CFO succession plan with explicit digital asset competence requirement
- Project cost: €38,000

**Lesson:** Concentrating access in one person is not security. It is operational risk.
